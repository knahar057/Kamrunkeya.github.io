Kamrun Nahar Keya

School of Mathematical and Statistical Sciences, Arizona State University

I am a Ph.D. student in Applied Mathematics at Arizona State University. My research interests lie in computational mathematics, optimization, matrix and tensor methods, numerical analysis, and data-driven modeling. I work on algorithms for low-rank matrix factorization, online optimization, randomized tensor decomposition, and mathematical models motivated by biological and applied systems.

Alongside research, I have extensive teaching and leadership experience. I currently serve as a Graduate Teaching Associate at Arizona State University and have held multiple student leadership roles, including President of the Association for Women in Mathematics student chapter and Treasurer of the SIAM student chapter. I am particularly interested in building mathematical tools that are both theoretically grounded and practically useful.

Brief Summary

My academic work spans optimization, numerical simulation, applied linear algebra, and tensor-based methods for large-scale and dynamic data settings. I am interested in research at the interface of computational mathematics, data science, and applied modeling.

I also have strong teaching and mentoring experience across calculus, pre-calculus, linear algebra, MATLAB, partial differential equations, and applied analysis, together with sustained leadership in student organizations and graduate community service.
